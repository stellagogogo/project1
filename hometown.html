Sanming Yong'an

Yong'an City, a county-level city under the jurisdiction of Fujian Province, is managed by Sanming City. It is located in the central and western part of Fujian Province, in the southern part of Sanming City, between the western and central mountainous areas of Fujian. It belongs to the subtropical maritime monsoon climate and is adjacent to 7 districts, counties (cities), with a total area of 2931 square kilometers.

Yong'an City is named after its symbolic meaning. In the third year of the Jingtai reign of the Ming Dynasty (1452), 13 districts of Shaxian were designated as counties and named "Yong'an", symbolizing permanent stability; Due to the confluence of Jiulong River and Baxi River in the west of the city, resembling a swallowtail, the urban section of Jiulong River is called Yanjiang, hence also known as "Yancheng".

Taoyuan Cave

Taoyuan Cave Scenic Area, located 10 kilometers north of Yong'an City and adjacent to the 205 National Highway in the Banzi Mountain, covers an area of 37 square kilometers. It is named after the Taohua River in the scenic area and is a national level scenic spot and AAAA level tourist attraction. Song and Li Gang wrote in a poem: 'The palm trees are a hundred miles away from the sandy creek, and the water and stones are called Little Wuyi.' Therefore, it is also known as' Little Wuyi '. With Taoyuan Cave entrance as the axis, it is divided into 5 tourist attractions including Taoyuan Cave, Baizhangyan, Geli, Xiuzhuwan, and Baolutan, with 73 tourist attractions. During the Wanli reign of the Ming Dynasty, it was converted into a tourist area and more than 10 pavilions and towers were built. In 1987, it was rated as one of the first provincial-level scenic spots along with the Hidden Stone Forest; In 1994, it was once again rated as the third batch of national key scenic spots.

Gongchuan

Gongchuan Town, formerly known as "Gongbao", is the only castle style ancient town in Fujian. Gongchuan has produced many literati and famous scholars from ancient times to the present, and there are many cultural relics protection units. Three provincial-level cultural relics protection units: Ancient City Wall, Huiqing Bridge, and Sun Gang Public Stack; There are more than ten municipal level cultural relics protection units, including Zhengshun Temple, Chen Family Ancestral Hall, Gujing, and Li Baojun's former residence. Gongchuan has a long history and a strong cultural atmosphere. Gongchuan was established as a town as early as the Tang Dynasty, and its cultural heritage has been passed down for over a thousand years. Since the Song Dynasty, there have been many famous people, and the Chen family enjoyed the reputation of "one school of dual sciences, nine sons and ten scholars". During the Ming and Qing dynasties, there were numerous successful candidates, including jinshi, juren, and gongsheng. In the late Ming Dynasty castle, there was a academy with a collection of 100000 books.

Anzhenbao

Anzhenbao, also known as "Chiguancheng", is located in Yangtou Village, Huainan Township, Yong'an City, Sanming City, Fujian Province. In the eleventh year of the Guangxu reign of the Qing Dynasty (1885), local gentry Chi Zhanrui and Chi Liantong, father and son, donated more than ten thousand yuan to build this earthen fortress due to the rampant banditry in the late Qing Dynasty. It took 14 years to complete. The area is approximately 10000 square meters, with a building area of 6000 square meters. Anzhenbao faces west and east, with a square front and a semi-circular rear. It is built along the mountain and gradually increases in height. The outer wall is rammed with thick stone and soil, and the eaves, door leaves, window mullions, column bases, arch of wooden architecture beams, Queti, etc. in the castle can be seen everywhere with carvings, colored paintings, clay sculptures and murals. Each hall is carved with stories of the Three Kingdoms, Water Margin, Journey to the West, and other stories from Nanmu Floating Carvings. The characters have different shapes and have high tourism and viewing value, reflecting the high wisdom and artistic skills of the working people at that time.

Yong'an Rice Cake

Kuai Tiao is a famous snack in Yong'an City, made by steaming and grinding rice slurry. There are various ways to eat it. The most common way of eating is to blanch the rice noodles in boiling water and put them into bone soup to make soup powder. They can also be served with vegetables and meat stir fry, or rolled into a tube shape and dipped in soy sauce with a fork. Such rice noodles are called "rice noodle tube" or "fork shaped rice cake".

Yong'an fork fruit

Chacha fruit is made by selecting rice with a long production period, finely processing and grinding pure slices, grinding them into pulp water, and then mixing the pulp water and pouring it into an iron disc. It floats on a large pot of boiling water and is steamed, rolled into a cylindrical shape, which is called the fruit barrel. It can be dipped in soy sauce, garlic, chili, sesame oil and other seasonings for consumption, or stir fried. Especially in summer, it can replace cold drinks and is a popular snack among urban and rural residents in Yong'an City.

Yong'an dried bamboo shoots

Min bamboo shoots, a specialty of Yong'an City, Fujian Province, China, and a national geographical indication product. The history of Yong'an Min bamboo shoots can be traced back to at least the Ming Dynasty. In the mid Ming Dynasty, with the migration of bamboo farmers from the Central Plains to the south, the production technology of bamboo shoots was introduced. The people of Yong'an combined the local bamboo shoot processing methods with it, and first invented and created the earliest prototype of processing technology for Min bamboo shoots. Min bamboo shoots are refined using traditional techniques such as shell removal, steaming, pressing, drying, and shaping, and are refined using scientific methods. After soaking and cooking, various dishes with refreshing and delicious taste can be made.
